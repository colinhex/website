Why Information Science?

I have always been interested in technology, and it did not take long for me to take up programming and mathematics as favorites in my studies. An entry point towards computer science for me was the realization that no matter the sub-subject or issue, time and sustained engagement can make a fool be a rookie and let a rookie become savvy. I discovered some affinity for structure and some sense of beauty in code. Be it in the predictability and elegance of algorithms or in the pragmatic and creative way of heuristics.

There are right ways to do things, by design or metrics and if you stumble across it by research or are told once you are sure not to make the same mistake twice, as it probably gave you more head-aches than you like to admit. If you are thorough enough and have a curious outlook that welcomes criticism you very likely prevent mistakes all-together. Being empathetic towards others, this makes results especially satisfying: knowing you worked on something that is persistent, secure and won’t cause head-aches.

Through the course of my degree I was dabbling first in Java, then MatLab and Python, then some C++ and SQL and, at the time of writing, in the basics of web-development. My biggest "Ahaa"-moment was not some deeply-buried-in-the-stack exception, though there were quite a few of those, but the sheer size of all individual and interconnected software-systems that span the globe and drive the economy, society and politics of today and tomorrow.

Hobbies and Interests

One major driver of my interest was always the ways authors could put far away futures into words. When I was young I was captivated by Isaac Asimov’s novels and not long ago by Adrian Tchaikovsky’s Children of Time, Liu Cixin’s Remembrance of Earths Past and, to kill the time, Pierce Brown’s Red Rising book series.

All the themes of fictitious shows, movies and books that tie me up have some scope to them, but I can also enjoy some action movie here and there. On the non-fiction side one of my favorite reads was The Algorithm Design Manual by Steven Skiena. When time is short I do like to listen to audiobooks while doing mundane every-day things to get my spirit up. It takes a good chunk of boredom out of the day and chores are much more rewarding.

To keep myself fit I work out and swim in my spare time. On weekends, I meet friends at bars or restaurants. So In short, you might meet me in public pools and places serving good food and drinks! If the weather is right and the time is there, skiing or traveling also cheer me up. Some on my bucket-list are the Rocky Mountains, the Red-Wood Forests, Moscow and Islands like Okinawa, the Canaries and the Maldives.

Other things I recently started practicing are sudoku, chess and Russian, with the intent to keep myself sharp and the effect of feeling like climbing steep hills below a fog that disillusions any sense of progress.
